Game of Thrones Fantasy Drama Set on the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, Game of Thrones has several plot lines and a large ensemble cast but centers on three primary story arcs. The first story arc centers on the Iron Throne of the Seven Kingdoms and follows a web of alliances and conflicts among the dynastic noble families either vying to claim the throne or fighting for independence from the throne. The second story arc focuses on the last descendant of the realm's deposed ruling dynasty, exiled and plotting a return to the throne. The third story arc centers on the longstanding brotherhood charged with defending the realm against the ancient threats of the fierce peoples and legendary creatures that lie far north, and an impending winter that threatens the realm. The Walking Dead Post-Apocalyptic Horror Series The Walking Dead takes place after the onset of a worldwide zombie apocalypse. The zombies, colloquially referred to as "walkers", shamble towards living humans and other creatures to eat them (they are attracted to noise, e.g., gunshots, and to different scents, e.g., humans). Humans that they bite or scratch become infected and slowly turn into walkers as well. It is revealed early in the series that all living humans carry this pathogen, so that if they die from any other cause, they will also turn into walkers. The only way to permanently kill a walker is to damage its brain or otherwise fully destroy the body, such as by cremating it. Modern Family Family Comedy Series Modern Family revolves around three different types of families (nuclear, step- and same-sex) living in the Los Angeles area who are interrelated through Jay Pritchett and his children, Claire Dunphy (née Pritchett) and Mitchell Pritchett. Patriarch Jay is remarried to a much younger woman, Gloria Delgado Pritchett (née Ramirez), a passionate[5] Colombian with whom he has an infant son, Fulgencio (Joe) Pritchett, and a son from Gloria's previous marriage,[6] Manny Delgado. Jay's daughter Claire was a homemaker, but has returned to the business world. She now works in her father's closet business. She is married to Phil Dunphy, a realtor and self-professed "cool Dad". They have three children: Haley Dunphy, a stereotypical ditzy teenage girl;[7] Alex Dunphy, a nerdy, smart middle child;[8] and Luke Dunphy, the off-beat only son.[9] Jay's lawyer son Mitchell and his husband Cameron Tucker have an adopted Vietnamese daughter, Lily Tucker-Pritchett. As the name suggests, this family represents a modern-day family and episodes are comically based on situations which many families encounter in real life.

Description

Owl Accordion is an Angular component that creates a simple accordion control to allow user to group different content in tabs.

Import

Expansion Panel

Each expansion panel must have a header and its panel content.

Header

owl-expansion-panel-header shows a summary of the panel's content and acts as the control of expanding and collapsing the panel. Each time the expansion panel's header is clicked, the expansion panel's content would be shown or hidden.

owl-expansion-panel-header may optionally contain an owl-expansion-panel-title and an owl-expansion-panel-description, which format the content of the header to align to different positions of the header.

By default, the expansion panel header would includes a toggle icon at the end of the header. Whenever the expansion state changes, the toggle icon would be animated to show whether the expansion panel is expanded or collapsed. Users could also use their own toggle icon by wrapping them with owl-expansion-panel-toggle. The toggle icon can also be hidden via the [hideToggle] property in owl-accordion.

Content

Expansion panel content must be wrapped inside owl-expansion-panel. By default, the expansion panel content will be initialised even when the panel is collapsed. To instead defer initialisation until the panel is expanded, the content should be provided as an ng-template with owlExpansionPanelContent directive.

Accordion

Multiple expansion panels owl-expansion-panel must be wrapped within an accordion owl-accordion. By default, only one panel can be expanded at a time. This behaviour can be changed by setting property [multiple] to true in owl-accordion. This would allow multiple expansion panels to be expanded at the same time.

owl-accordion

Properties

Name Type Optional Default Description
hideToggle false Optional false Whether to hide the expansion toggle icon.
multiple false Optional false Whether to allow multi expansion panels to be expanded at the same time.
accordionMode 'gutter' | 'flat' Optional 'gutter' The accordion mode used for all expansion panels.
There are two display mode:
  • gutter -- a gutter-like spacing is placed around any expanded panel, placing the expanded panel at a different elevation from the reset of the accordion.
  • flat -- no spacing is placed around expanded panels, showing all panels at the same elevation.

owl-expansion-panel

Properties

Name Type Optional Default Description
expanded boolean optional false Whether the expansion panel is expanded.
disabled boolean optional false Whether the expansion panel is disabled.
hideToggle false Optional false Whether to hide the expansion toggle icon.

Events

Name Parameters Description
opened null Emits when the expansion panel is opened.
closed null Emits when the expansion panel is closed.

owl-expansion-panel-header

Properties

Name Type Optional Default Description
expandedHeight string optional '4em' Defines the height of the header while the panel is expanded.
collapsedHeight string optional '3em' Defines the height of the header while the panel is collapsed.
hideToggle false Optional false Whether to hide the expansion toggle icon.
